Our Motive

We provide a clear understanding of the prerequisite basic terms required in mechanical and automation.

Engineering Graphics

- Engineering Drawing: A graphical language used by engineers and architects to communicate ideas and details of a product through drawings.
- B.I.S. Specifications: Standards set by the Bureau of Indian Standards, providing guidelines for the quality and safety of products.
- Engineering Curves: Curves used in engineering drawings to represent various shapes and profiles.
- Projections: The representation of a three-dimensional object on a two-dimensional surface.
- Sections of Solids: Representations of cross-sectional views of three-dimensional objects.
- Development of Surfaces: Unfolding or unrolling the surfaces of three-dimensional objects to represent them in two dimensions.

Engineering Mechanics

- Laws of Mechanics: Fundamental principles governing the motion and equilibrium of objects.
- Force Systems: Collections of forces acting on an object.
- Equilibrium Conditions: Conditions under which an object is in a state of balance.
- Laws of Dry Friction: Principles describing the frictional force between dry surfaces.
- Centroids and Centre of Gravity: Geometric centers of shapes and distributions of mass, respectively.
- Kinematics: The study of motion without considering the forces causing the motion.
- Kinetics: The study of motion taking into account the forces causing the motion.

Numerical Techniques for Engineers

- Floating-Point Numbers: A numerical representation that uses a fixed number of digits for the representation of real numbers.
- Non-Linear Equations: Equations that do not have a linear relationship between the variables.
- LU Decomposition: A method of decomposing a matrix into the product of a lower triangular matrix and an upper triangular matrix.

Production Technology II

- Machine Tools: Tools and machines used for shaping and machining materials.
- Gears: Mechanical components with teeth that transmit motion and power.
- Bearings: Mechanical components that support and reduce friction in the motion of other components.

Theory of Machines

- Kinematics: The study of motion, without considering the forces causing the motion.
- Cams: Mechanical components that impart motion to other parts through a predetermined profile.
- Vibrations: Oscillatory motions, often undesirable in machinery.

Thermal Engineering II

- Reciprocating Compressors: Machines that compress gases through reciprocating motion.
- Centrifugal Compressors: Machines that compress gases using centrifugal force.
- Gas Turbines: Engines that convert gas energy into mechanical energy.

Workshop Practices

- Workshop Practices: Activities and procedures carried out in a workshop for manufacturing or repairing products.
- Safety Precautions: Measures taken to ensure the well-being of individuals working in a workshop.
- Foundry Shop: A facility where metal casting processes, such as sand molding, are carried out.
- Fitting Operations: Operations involving the assembly and fitting of different components.
- Welding: Joining process that involves melting and fusing materials.

Production Technology I

- Casting Processes: Various methods of forming metal objects by pouring molten metal into molds.
- Welding Processes: Techniques of joining metals by melting and fusing them together.
- Powder Metallurgy: A manufacturing process that produces metal parts from metal powders.
- Strength of Materials: The study of the behavior of materials under different types of loads and stresses.

Thermal Engineering I

- Thermodynamics: The study of energy and its transformations, particularly in relation to heat and work.
- Gas Power Cycles: Processes that involve the compression and expansion of gases to produce power.
- Steam Properties: Characteristics and behaviors of steam, a common working fluid in thermodynamic systems.

h3>Machine Design

- Design Process: The systematic and iterative process of creating a product or system.
- Material Properties: Characteristics of materials relevant to their performance in engineering applications.
- Fasteners: Devices used to join or secure components together.

Fluid Mechanics and Hydraulic Mechanics

- Fluid Properties: Characteristics of fluids, including liquids and gases.
- Pressure Measurement Techniques: Methods for measuring the pressure of fluids.
- Hydrostatics: The study of fluids at rest.
- Kinematics of Fluids: The study of fluid motion without considering the forces causing the motion.
- Integral and Differential Analysis: Basic laws and control volume analysis. Differential analysis and Navier-Stokes equations.
- Internal and External Flow: Flow through pipes and boundary layers. Performance characteristics of turbines and pumps.

Automobile Engineering

- Vehicle Construction: The design and assembly of automotive vehicles.
- Resistance Characteristics: Factors influencing the resistance faced by a vehicle in motion.
- Transmission Systems: Mechanisms for transmitting power from the engine to the wheels.
- Suspension Systems: Components that support and isolate the vehicle from shocks.
- Crankshafts, Pistons, Valves: Engine components involved in the combustion process.
- Electric Vehicles: Vehicles powered by electric motors, often using rechargeable batteries.
